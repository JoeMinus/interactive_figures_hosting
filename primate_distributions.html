Software & data

Map coastline data: Kocsis, Á.T. and Scotese, C.R., 2021. Mapping paleocoastlines and continental flooding during the Phanerozoic. Earth-Science Reviews, 213, p.103463.

Map style edited in: QGIS.org, 2026. QGIS Geographic Information System. QGIS Association. http://www.qgis.org

Phylogeny: Adapted from the metatree approach that combines genetic and morphological data in a single analysis. Wisniewski, A.L., Lloyd, G.T. and Slater, G.J., 2022. Extant species fail to estimate ancestral geographical ranges at older nodes in primate phylogeny. Proceedings of the Royal Society B: Biological Sciences, 289(1975).

Plotting the phylogeny: To plot the phylogeny I used TreeViewer. See here for more details: Bianchini, G., & Sánchez-Baracaldo, P. (2024). TreeViewer: Flexible, modular software to visualise and manipulate phylogenetic trees. Ecology and Evolution, 14, e10873. https://doi.org/10.1002/ece3.10873

Bleeding tree effect: Designed in Inkscape.

Fossil data (and also great paper): Based on Avaria-Llautureo, J., Püschel, T.A., Meade, A., Baker, J., Nicholson, S.L. and Venditti, C., 2025. The radiation and geographic expansion of primates through diverse climates. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 122(32), p.e2423833122.

Taxonomic images: Sourced from https://www.phylopic.org/

AI usage: Claude and ChatGPT for helping code the html. No AI used for writing, research, or art design.
