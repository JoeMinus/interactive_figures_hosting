## Welcome!
The Earth we see today is just a single snapshot from an album that stretches back 4 billion years. Every ecosystem, the climate, the types of animals that live there, their shapes, their genetics
their behaviours, even the shape of the continents themselves, all have been touched by this unfathomably rich Earthly lore. By scrutinizing these features, we can attempt to understand the force
that shaped them and piece together the story of life throughout geological time. The lens is often muddy, and it typically gets harder to see the farther back in time we go, but the ceaseles
determination of scientists continues to improve our understanding against all of the odds.

This resource is an attempt to summarize our current understanding of the evolution of mammals throughout the Cenozoic (the geological period that follows the extinciton of the nonavian dinosuars an
ends with... well it still hasn't!) Few events in Earth's history provide a better example of how our modern world follows as a consequence of the past. Mammals existed before the meteorite but the
showed little of the extreme morphological diversity that we expect of mammals today. The largest animal that has ever lived (the blue whale), is a mammal. Yet in the days of the dinosaurs, no mamma
got much bigger than a badger. If it were not for the meteor, dinosaurs may still reign and we may have not existed at all.

We will start with the Primates. As you yourself are a primate, this not only to satisfy your narcissistic tendencies, but also because Primates provide a perfect example of how we can use pattern
in genes, the fossil record, and the distribution of living species today to uncover truly shocking events that occurred in Earth's history. 

We will start before the asteroid hit, before the origination of primates proper. By clicking the arrows below, you can move from time slice to time slice.
